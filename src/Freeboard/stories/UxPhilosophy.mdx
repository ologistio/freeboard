# UX philosophy

This is the reasoning behind Freeboard's interface. The rules page
(Foundations > UX rules) turns each principle into a check you can apply in
review; this page explains the why, so the rules are applied with judgment
rather than by rote.

Freeboard's job is to make a compliance posture legible and defensible: evidence
collected automatically, checks scored against it, and a trail an auditor can
trust. The interface either makes that legible or buries it. These principles
keep it legible.

## Failure modes we design against

Every principle below answers a way compliance tooling goes wrong as it grows.

- **Nav accretion.** Modules sprout their own settings and report pages until the
  menu is a museum of past decisions.
- **Disagreeing task surfaces.** Two lists of "what to do" that show different
  counts, so neither is trusted.
- **Duplicate records.** The same piece of evidence shown as two unrelated things
  on two pages, each needing its own explanation.
- **Notification noise.** So many alerts that the signal disappears and monitoring
  becomes background radiation.
- **Overwhelm and depth-hiding.** Important flows buried inside a specific page,
  unreachable from the main menu.
- **Opaque derived numbers.** A score that jumps from bad to good in one step and
  reflects nothing in between.
- **Silent failure.** An upload that half-worked, discovered later by someone else.

## Principles

### One object, many lenses

Evidence and the checks it satisfies are the primary objects. Frameworks,
scorecards, and reports are lenses over those shared objects, not parallel copies.
Fixing a gap once updates every place that references it: fix once, count
everywhere. A framework page is a progress view over shared controls, never a
separate checklist to maintain by hand.

### One work item model

Everything actionable - a failing check, a policy approval, a review item, an
expiring piece of evidence - is the same task shape: an owner, a due date or SLA,
and a source object. The program queue and the personal queue are the same ledger
at two scopes, so their counts can never disagree for the same scope.

### Exceptions first, health collapsed

Default sorts put failing and due-soon items on top. Passing items earn their
silence. A gaps-only view is a first-class control, not a filter buried in a menu.
The daily face of the product is the short list of things that are wrong, not the
long list of things that are fine.

### Provenance on every automated value

Every machine-collected value carries a source stamp: which collector produced it,
and how fresh it is. A stale or degraded source degrades its dependent checks
visibly, with the dependency named. Nothing passes because its source went quiet,
and nothing fails without saying why.

### Internal and external surfaces are visually distinct

The public trust surface is the only outward-facing part of the product, and it is
framed so it can never be mistaken for an internal page: a persistent
public-surface banner, publish-after-approval, and a publish log. Auditor and other
external views carry the same frame. Nothing leaves the building by accident.

### Two levels of navigation, no more

Group, then page. Anything deeper is a tab or a drawer on a page. Settings and
reporting are each one surface, sectioned by module, never one page per module.
Cross-cutting concerns - search, tasks, notifications, the assistant - live in
exactly one place each.

### Honest derived numbers

A derived score moves on a scale with its inputs and shows its derivation on
demand: which inputs, which weights. It never flips from bad to good in a single
binary step, because a real posture is rarely binary and the interface should not
pretend otherwise.

### Errors and emptiness are instructions

A failed action states what failed, to which item, and the next step. An empty
page teaches its purpose and offers the first action. Confusion is answered where
it happens - an inline hint or a guidance box - not in a separate help article
about the relationship between two pages.

## How to use this

The rules page is the enforceable form of these principles: numbered, testable in
review. When a rule and a principle appear to conflict, the principle governs and
the rule is probably missing a case. Raise it, and we extend the ruleset. This is
a living record, not a tablet.
